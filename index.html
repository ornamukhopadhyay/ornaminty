  • 1920 Born to a British-Jewish family.
  • Her family had hosted Jewish refugees from Nazi Germany.
  • Went to St. Paul's Girls School. Fluent in French + German, topped classes, and won annual awards. Won a scholarship, but gave to refugee students.
  • 1938 Started studying chemistry at Cambridge + spectroscopic techniques under Dr. Bill Price. Also met with Adrienne Weill in last year @ Cambridge, who was a student of Marie Curie + had a huge influence on life.
  • 1942 After a failed year in a lab post-graduation, she worked as an assistant research officer for Britain during WW2 + with cousin Irene, was an Air Raid Warden.
  • 1945 Got her PHd Thesis on The physical chemistry of solid organic colloids with special reference to coal.
  • 1946 Worked in Paris on X-ray crystallography of amorphous substances with Jacques Mering.
  • 1950-53 Started working in King's College, London, @ Biophysics Unit on X-ray diffraction on proteins + nucleic acids. Found helical shape in 1951, but continued checking. She continued working on A-DNA and B-DNA, and it was revealed that she was the first to see the double helix in her manuscripts. Watson and Crick barely gave any credit to her or Wilkins in their 1953 Nature publication.
  • 1953-1958 Went to Birbeck College, and worked on RNA crystallography w/ the Tobacco Mosaic Virus (TMV) as well as the polio virus.
  • 1956 Started undergoing cancer treatments.
  • 1958 Died of (most likely) ovarian cancer, mainly because of prolonged exposure to X-ray radiation.
"[Franklin] came very much closer to the discovery of the double helix than she has usually been credited with doing."
Anne Sayre. Rosalind Franklin and DNA. 1975.
